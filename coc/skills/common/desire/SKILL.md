# desire

## Skill Kind

- cognition-node

## Node Role

- Infer what the character wants or prefers from their goals, priorities, and stated/implicit preferences.
- Separate a true desire from social obligation, habit, or another person's preference.

## When To Use

- Use for multiple-desire, discrepant-desire, and preference-conflict items.
- Use when the character expresses, hints at, or asserts a want, choice, or priority.

## Required Inputs

- Triggering event or invitation summary
- Question
- Each party's stated interests or prior pattern

## Output Contract

- Whose desire is in question
- The goal or preference at stake
- Most likely wanted option
- Closest wrong alternative to reject

## Methodology

- Identify each party's stated interest or prior behavioural pattern.
- Check for an explicit assertion of preference in the current scene.
- Prefer the option grounded in the character's own stated want over a default assumption or a compromise.
- Keep the answer tied to the exact wording of the question.

## Pitfalls

- Do not assume everyone shares the same preference.
- Do not pick a compromise option when the story explicitly asserts one party's own desire.
- Do not confuse a social obligation or stated norm with a genuine want.

## Discrepant-Desire Assertion Rule

When the story says 'X always gives way to Y BUT this time / tonight / today X expresses he/she wants Z':
- The 'but this time' clause BREAKS the historic compromise pattern.
- The answer is X's preferred Z, not Y's preference, and not a compromise activity.
- NEVER pick a compromise option when the story explicitly says X is asserting his/her own desire today.

## Invite-Intersection Rule (Multiple Desires)

When X (with interest A) invites Y (with interest B, and Y is not interested in A):
- The activity X picks is the option that COMBINES A and B in a single phrase.
- ALWAYS reject pure-A and pure-B options when an intersection option exists.
- Common intersection markers in the option text: '...combined with...', 'tech-themed [history thing]', 'digital [traditional thing]', '[A] of [B]'.

## Switch Rules

- If the desire depends on a mistaken belief, add `belief-state` first.
- If the question asks how the desire makes the character feel, add `emotion`.
- If the question asks what the desire leads the person to do, add `intent-strategy` or `action-forecast`.

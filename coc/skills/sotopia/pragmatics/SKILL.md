# pragmatics

## Skill Kind

- cognition-node

## Node Role

- Decode the pragmatic meaning of the partner's last statement in the Sotopia context.
- Identify what the partner is implying, hedging, or protecting through indirect language.
- Use that reading to choose a response that matches the true communicative intent.

## When To Use

- Use when the partner's statement seems indirect, polite-evasive, or non-committal.
- Use when the partner uses face-saving language that could mean yes or no.
- Use when a refusal is wrapped in excessive politeness.
- Use when the partner changes the subject in a way that might signal discomfort.
- Use for repair situations where the partner's statement implies hurt without stating it.

## Required Inputs

- Partner's exact last utterance
- Relationship context (stranger / acquaintance / close friend / adversary)
- Tone and social stakes of the interaction

## Output Contract

- Literal meaning: what was said
- Pragmatic meaning: what was implied or meant
- Face concern: what the partner was trying to protect
- Best response register: direct / indirect / validating / clarifying
- Pragmatic pitfall to avoid (e.g., calling out the indirectness too bluntly)

## Methodology

- Read the partner's last turn for hedges, qualifiers, topic shifts, and excessive politeness.
- Infer the underlying communicative function: request, refusal, concern, test, softened demand.
- Assess the face stakes: is the partner protecting their own face, the agent's face, or the relationship?
- Choose a response register that matches the pragmatic level — do not force explicit direct language when the partner is signaling indirection is required.
- If the partner's indirect signal is ambiguous, prefer a reply that works for both interpretations.

## Pitfalls

- Do not treat indirect language as straightforward agreement.
- Do not respond to a polite refusal as if it were a yes.
- Do not expose the partner's face-saving move explicitly in the reply — respond to the intent, not the evasion.
- Do not over-interpret ordinary politeness as deep subtext.
- Do not confuse uncertainty with a hidden agenda.

## Switch Rules

- If the pragmatic reading reveals emotional distress, add `emotion`.
- If the implied meaning changes the tactic, add `intent-strategy`.
- If the agent's reply needs to match the indirectness level, add `action-forecast`.
